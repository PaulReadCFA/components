◈ Exploration Questions

A bond has a face value of $1,000, an annual coupon rate of 5%, and matures in 3 years. The market discount rate is 4%.

What is the fair value (price) of this bond?

Discount each cash flow at 4%:

\[ P = \frac{50}{1.04} + \frac{50}{1.04^2} + \frac{1050}{1.04^3} \approx 1027.75 \]

The bond trades at a premium because the coupon rate (5%) exceeds the discount rate (4%).

Same bond — face value $1,000, 5% annual coupon, 3 years to maturity. The market discount rate is now 6%.

How does the price change compared to Question 1, and why?

\[ P = \frac{50}{1.06} + \frac{50}{1.06^2} + \frac{1050}{1.06^3} \approx 973.35 \]

The price falls below par — the coupon rate (5%) is now less than the discount rate (6%). This demonstrates the inverse relationship between bond prices and yields.

A zero-coupon bond has a face value of $1,000, matures in 5 years, and the market discount rate is 3%.

What should an investor pay for this bond today?

\[ \mathrm{PV} = \frac{1000}{(1.03)^5} \approx 862.61 \]

No intermediate coupons — the $137.39 difference between purchase price and face value is the investor's total return.

Two bonds are identical except for maturity: Bond A matures in 2 years, Bond B in 10 years. Both have a 5% annual coupon and $1,000 face value. The market discount rate rises from 5% to 6%.

Which bond falls more in price? What principle does this illustrate?

Bond A at 6%: $981.67 → falls 1.83%

Bond B at 6%: $926.40 → falls 7.36%

Bond B falls much further, illustrating duration risk: longer-maturity bonds are more sensitive to rate changes because a larger share of their value comes from distant cash flows.

Implementation notes

CSS only · Font Awesome

These use the same CFA Program box callouts as demo-library — section 6 (cfa-callout--shaded, --exercise, --discussion). Titles describe this page; modifiers match the curriculum menu.

No extra JS

The .cfa-exploration component uses the existing tab and expand behaviour from cfa-components.js. Nothing new to add.

Unique IDs per page

If two .cfa-exploration blocks appear on the same Canvas page, give each its own ID prefix — e.g. eq2-t1 / eq2-p1 — to avoid duplicate IDs breaking ARIA associations.

Voice control

Each Check Answer button has a unique aria-label ("Check answer for Question 1", etc.) so Dragon NaturallySpeaking and Voice Control users can target specific buttons even though the visible text is identical.
